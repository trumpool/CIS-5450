5450 Presentation

## Overview
The purpose of the presentation is to give you a chance to practice communicating your data science project to stakeholders, which is an invaluable skill to have. We can spend hours cleaning the data and fine-tuning the models, but, if potential stakeholders cannot understand the value of the results or use the results to formulate actionable strategies, the modeling effort may not receive the attention it deserves.

This is an incredibly challenging skill to master. For this project, it may be helpful to think of a potential stakeholder that may find your project interesting or impactful. And, you have 10 mins to pitch the problem statement, method/model, and implications/finding/results to them!

Every group must prepare and deliver a recorded 8-10 mins presentation (no more, no less) that provides a summary of your project. Presentations can optionally be delivered live as well. Please include the following details:

- Objective and value proposition
- Dataset used
- Major learnings from EDA (with top 3-5 well-formatted charts that deepened your understanding of the dataset and informed your model)
- Modeling results (models used, performance, etc)
- Hypothesis test results (if any)
- Implications and insights
- Challenges/limitations/potential future work

## Presentation Expectations
- You should use slides to present the content requested. There are different slide-making applications, feel free to use any that you feel comfortable with.
- You’ll be asked to submit your slides as a PDF as well for reference.
- Please do not include code. 
- The face of the team member speaking must always be present onscreen. Additionally, only your voices can be used for audio - no text-to-speech software is allowed.

## Rubric
Along with your annotated notebook, you will also be required to submit a presentation to the TAs and professors grading your project. Presentations can be done via recorded video or live if you would like.Requirements for video presentations: The face of the person speaking must be visible, and you are required to use your own voices for audio.

### Required components
- Objective, value proposition, and dataset used
- Major learnings from EDA (with top 3-5 well-formatted charts that deepened your understanding of the dataset and informed your model)
- Modeling results (models used, performance, etc)
- Implications and insights
- Challenges/limitations/potential future work

Not all group members are required to speak during the presentation. Everyone should still be in the video and visible, but only one person has to speak.
